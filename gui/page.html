BioMassPredict

Hierarchical Biomass Prediction Framework

A state-of-the-art research plan tailored for agricultural biomass estimation. Integrating Computer Vision with Tabular Metadata to solve zero-inflation and hierarchical consistency challenges.

1

The Challenge

Predicting biomass components (Green, Dead, Clover) that often sum to zero (sparse data) or have strict hierarchical relationships.

2

The Solution

An ensemble of DINOv2 and EfficientNetV2, utilizing knowledge distillation from privileged features (NDVI) and Tweedie loss.

3

The Outcome

Robust predictions constrained by physical reality ($Total = \sum Parts$) with high Weighted $R^2$ performance.

Target Variable Hierarchy

Understanding the output constraints. Hover over the chart to see component details.

The model doesn't just predict one number. It must simultaneously predict five distinct components while ensuring the sum matches the total biomass. This is known as a Hierarchical Constraint.

  • Green: Live photosynthesizing matter.
  • Dead: Senescent material.
  • Clover: Specific legume species.
  • Weeds/Litter: Other biological components.

Data Strategy: The pipeline combines high-resolution RGB imagery with tabular site metadata. Crucially, "Privileged Information" (like NDVI or precise Height) is used during training via distillation but is not required for inference.

Training Strategy: Tweedie Loss

Biomass data is heavily "zero-inflated" (many samples have 0 clover or 0 dead mass). Standard MSE loss fails here. We use Tweedie Loss (Compound Poisson-Gamma) with power parameter $p \in (1, 2)$.

  • ‚úî Models both the probability of zero mass and the continuous distribution of positive mass.
  • ‚úî Weighted R¬≤ is used for evaluation to penalize errors in large biomass values more heavily.

Formula Snippet

Loss = -y * \mu^(1-p)/(1-p) + \mu^(2-p)/(2-p)
